Main Topic/Objective
The call was a sales pitch by Pooja (representing Rice) to a prospective investor (referred to as “sir”) for a commercial retail shop in a new mall project planned on the Gurgaon‑Delhi border (Upper Dwar Expressway). The goal was to explain the investment terms and gauge the client’s interest in proceeding.

Key Points Discussed
- Project Overview: A mall will be constructed in Gurgaon over the next three years. Investors will be allocated individual shop spaces (150 sq ft) that will be rented out once the mall is operational.
- Investment Offer: Total cost of the shop is INR 50 lakh. The investor must pay 60 % (INR 30 lakh) upfront; the remaining 40 % can be paid after construction is complete (approximately 2–2.5 years).
- Guaranteed Returns: An assured interest of 12 % per annum on the amount paid upfront, payable during the construction phase. After completion, the investor will receive the full rental income generated from the shop (referred to as “lifetime rental”).
- Communication Preference: The client asked for the full details to be explained in Hindi; Pooja attempted to clarify the proposal in Hindi but the client indicated limited understanding.
- Next Steps: Pooja offered to send the complete project details (location, plan, financials) to the client for review.

Decisions Made / Action Items
- Pooja will email (or otherwise send) the detailed proposal, including location map, shop layout, payment schedule, and return calculations, to the client.
- The client will review the materials and decide whether to proceed with the 60 % upfront payment.
- Follow‑up call: Pooja will call the client after the details are sent to answer any further questions and discuss the investment commitment.
