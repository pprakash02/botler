Main Topic/Objective
The parent (referred to as “ma’am”) called the university’s admission office to find out why her son’s DMC (Degree/Mark Certificate) for the 6th and 7th semesters of the B‑Pharmacy program (2021‑2025) has not been issued, which is blocking his registration.

Key Points Discussed
- The student, Lokesh, son of Jaisingh, completed the B‑Pharmacy course and passed the re‑exams for semesters 6 and 7, but the DMC has not been printed or released.  
- Because the DMC is missing, the university cannot complete his registration.  
- The parent has already visited the campus several times; each time staff said the DMC would be ready, but it still has not been received.  
- The admission officer explained that the DMC status is handled by the department, not the admission cell, but offered to help since the call was made.  
- The officer asked the parent to send the student to the “Adam Block” (admissions office) and to provide the student’s roll number and other details so the matter can be checked.  
- The parent supplied the following identifiers: student name Lokesh, father’s name Jaisingh, program B‑Pharmacy 2021‑2025, and roll numbers/IDs 215, 364, 374.  
- The officer agreed to note the information, verify the DMC status, and call back the next day with an update.

Decisions Made / Action Items
- The parent will take Lokesh to the Adam Block (admissions office) to inquire in person.  
- The admission officer will record the student’s details (name, father’s name, program, roll numbers) and forward the request to the relevant department.  
- The admission officer will check whether the DMC has been generated and will contact the parent the following day to confirm the outcome.
